HushAsk vs Lattice: Performance Reviews vs Anonymous Feedback

Lattice and HushAsk operate on different timescales — one manages quarterly review cycles, the other handles the Tuesday-afternoon conversation that does not fit a cycle. A practical breakdown of where each one wins.

Morgan — Content Lead, HushAsk Morgan writes about workplace communication, employee feedback, and the systems that make honesty possible. She covers HR technology for the HushAsk blog.

Lattice's category: performance management

Lattice is a performance-management platform built around the formal review cycle. The product covers goal-setting and OKR tracking, weekly one-on-one templates, performance reviews with calibration support, growth plans, career path documents, and the analytics dashboards that let People Ops teams track participation and outcomes across the org. The customer is typically a 100+ person company where HR has decided that performance management deserves its own software, separate from the engagement-survey program and the payroll system.

The thing Lattice does best is making a performance review cycle run on rails. The product knows what stage each manager and IC are in, who has not yet submitted, what calibration sessions are scheduled, and what the final ratings distribution looks like across teams. For a People Ops lead trying to land a review cycle without it becoming a six-week swamp, the operational lift this provides is real. The growth-plan layer — letting an IC document a career path and check progress against it — is another piece of the program that benefits from being structured software rather than a quarterly document everyone forgets.

The product is also, importantly, named and cyclical. Reviews happen on a calendar. Goals are attached to people. One-on-ones have an owner and a participant. The shape of the program is HR-led, calendar-driven, and identity-attached.

HushAsk's category: continuous Q&A

HushAsk is the conversation that lives between the cycles Lattice manages. The product is anonymous, two-way Q&A in Slack. There is no review cycle. There is no goal-tracking. There is no calibration. The shape is a single Slack channel where a leader receives anonymous messages and replies anonymously in-thread.

The architectural difference is the anonymity model. With HushAsk, the Slack user identifier is replaced with a 64-character SHA-256 hash at send time. On Free, Pro, and Business+ Slack plans there is no path back to the original sender. (On Slack Enterprise Grid plans, Slack's own audit log records bot DM activity as a separate layer — a constraint HushAsk cannot suppress; the team should know that going in.) Lattice, by contrast, is identity-attached throughout — that is the design center for performance-management software, and the right design center for that category.

The two products operate on different timescales. Lattice runs on a quarterly or semi-annual cadence — review cycles, goal cycles, calibration. HushAsk runs on conversation timescale — a message can arrive on a Tuesday afternoon and get a reply that same day. The two timescales are not interchangeable, and most teams need both.

When to pick Lattice

Pick Lattice when the shape of what you need is structured performance management. You are running a formal review cycle and want the operational scaffolding around it. You need a system of record for goals and OKRs that ties into reviews. You have a People Ops function that is accountable for review participation and outcome quality. You want calibration sessions supported by software rather than spreadsheets. You want one-on-one notes to live somewhere durable, not in a notebook that the manager closes after the meeting.

Lattice is also the right tool when the company has growth-plan and career-path expectations that need to be documented. Engineering ladders, manager growth tracks, IC progression — Lattice has product surface for all of these in a way HushAsk does not pretend to. For a 100+ person company running a formal People Ops program, this is essential software, and a small Slack-based product does not substitute.

The honest answer for these use cases: pick Lattice. HushAsk is not in the category.

When to pick HushAsk

Pick HushAsk when the conversation you need is the one Lattice does not capture. A specific IC has something to raise on a Tuesday — about their manager, about the new architecture decision, about a team norm — that will not fit into the review cycle and is too specific for the quarterly engagement survey. The IC needs a place to send the message anonymously and get a response from a leader, this week, without scheduling a meeting or filing a ticket.

That is the gap. It is not a gap Lattice was designed to fill, and it is not a gap any tool in the performance-management category fills cleanly. Lattice has anonymous-feedback surfaces built into the review cycle — meaning the conversation happens once a quarter, in the context of a review, attached to a specific Lattice instrument. That is useful for what it is. It is not the same as an always-on anonymous channel.

The pattern that works for teams running both: Lattice for the cycle work — goals, reviews, growth plans, calibration — and HushAsk for the ad-hoc conversation that does not fit any of those instruments. The two products do not overlap. The mistake is treating either one as the whole feedback program. Lattice handles the structured, identity-attached, calendar-driven side. HushAsk handles the unstructured, anonymous, conversation-driven side. Both have a job. Neither does the other one's job.
